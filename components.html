Micro:bit Components

Click on each card to learn more about the components of Micro:bit

LED Display

The Micro:bit has 25 programmable LED lights that can display text and symbols.

Accelerometer

The built-in accelerometer detects motion and can sense changes in position.

Buttons

The Micro:bit has two programmable buttons to control programs or interact with games.

Compass

The built-in compass detects the direction you're facing and can sense magnetic fields.

Radio

The radio feature allows the Micro:bit to communicate wirelessly with other devices.

Microphone

The microphone allows you to capture sound and respond to noise levels in your programs.

Temperature Sensor

The built-in temperature sensor can detect the current temperature of the surroundings.

Light Sensor

The light sensor measures the amount of light around the Micro:bit and can trigger responses.

Bluetooth

The Bluetooth feature allows the Micro:bit to connect and communicate with mobile devices.

Edge Connector

The 25-pin edge connector allows you to connect external components like sensors, motors, and more.

Reset Button

A reset button at the back of the board to restart the Micro:bit without turning it off.

I2C and SPI Interfaces

Used to connect external devices using standard communication protocols.

USB Interface

Used for connecting the Micro:bit to a computer for power and programming.

Power Supply Connector (JST)

A connector for powering the Micro:bit with a battery pack.

Ground (GND)

The GND (ground) pin is used to complete circuits when connecting external components.
